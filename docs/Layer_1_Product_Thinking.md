# Layer 1: Product Thinking

## User Research Summary

### Methodology
- **Target Audience:** College students (Sophomores to Seniors)
- **Method:** Semi-structured interviews (simulated/planned for 20 students, 5 recent grads, 3 recruiters)

### Core Pain Points
1. **The "What should I do?" Paralysis:** Students don't know what skills are currently in-demand vs. outdated.
2. **Resume Blackhole:** Students feel their resumes are never seen by human eyes and don't know how ATS systems filter them.
3. **Project Uncertainty:** Unsure if their "To-Do List App" or "Weather App" is enough to crack an internship.
4. **Interview Anxiety:** Lack of realistic mock interview practice tailored to specific job descriptions.

## User Personas

### Persona 1: The Confused Sophomore (Alex)
- **Background:** CS major, knows basic Python and Java.
- **Pain Point:** Doesn't know what specialization to pick (Web Dev vs. AI vs. Data Science) and what to build first.
- **Needs:** A clear roadmap from point A to point B.

### Persona 2: The Anxious Senior (Priya)
- **Background:** Has done a few projects, applying to 100+ jobs, getting zero callbacks.
- **Pain Point:** Resume is likely poorly formatted or lacking impact metrics. Fails technical interviews due to anxiety.
- **Needs:** Brutal resume roast and realistic mock interview practice.

### Persona 3: The Recruiter (David)
- **Background:** Screens 500+ resumes a week for a tech firm.
- **Pain Point:** Tired of seeing the same generic projects and keyword-stuffed skills sections without context.
- **Needs:** Wants to see clear impact, relevant skills, and candidates who can actually communicate their technical choices.
